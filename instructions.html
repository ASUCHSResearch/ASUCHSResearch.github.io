How To Use This App

Use the poster companion on its own, or pair it with the Custom GPT if you have ChatGPT access.

Back to directory

This page is for attendees scanning QR codes at CHS Research Day. The app works without ChatGPT. The GPT buttons are optional.

Start Here

If you just scanned a poster QR code

  1. Open the poster page.
  2. Read the 30-second summary, key numbers, takeaways, and questions to ask.
  3. If that is enough, stay in the app. You do not need ChatGPT to use it.
  4. If you want help asking better questions or getting a quick explanation, use the GPT tools at the top of the poster page.

GPT Workflow

How to use the GPT tools

  1. Choose a prompt style such as Quick overview, Smart questions, or Plain language.
  2. Pick the audience and tone you want.
  3. Tap Copy prompt.
  4. Tap Open GPT.
  5. Paste the prompt into the Custom GPT chat and send it.

No ASU ChatGPT Account?

If you are an ASU employee or ASU student and do not have access yet

  1. Go to asu.edu/selfsub.
  2. Sign in with your ASURITE credentials.
  3. Scroll to the bottom and select Subscribe next to ChatGPT: User Account.
  4. Watch your ASU email for the invitation. ASU says this can take up to 24 hours.
  5. After that, go to chatgpt.com and sign in with ASURITE SSO.
ASU’s ChatGPT Edu page says all students, faculty, researchers, and staff have no-cost access to ChatGPT Edu with GPT-5. Student access is semester-based on request, and faculty/staff receive a full-year allocation.

If You Still Cannot Get In

What to do next

  • Keep using this app’s static poster companion. It is designed to work without ChatGPT.
  • Ask the presenter one of the built-in questions on the page.
  • If you are ASU and need help with access, use the ASU AI guidance page or contact the ASU Experience Center.

Best Use Cases

When the Custom GPT helps most

  • You want three better questions to ask the presenter.
  • You want a plain-language explanation for a student, family member, or community partner.
  • You want help understanding why the poster matters.
  • You want a respectful way to explore next steps or a collaboration angle.

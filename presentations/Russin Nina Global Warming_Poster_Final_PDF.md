## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster proposes a socio-ecological framework to explain why older adults are increasingly vulnerable to heat illness as global warming intensifies and populations age. It argues that effective mitigation requires interventions at multiple levels—patient education, community-wide warning systems, social support, and structural redesign for mobility and safe, affordable housing. The poster highlights physiological changes of aging that reduce heat tolerance, distinguishes classical vs exertional heat stroke, and notes medication classes (and NSAIDs/alcohol) that may impair heat tolerance.

## **P10. Glossary**

* **Thermoregulation:** The body’s ability to maintain core temperature; described as less effective with aging.  
* **Heat stroke (classical):** Hot, dry skin with rectal temperature \>104°F; can lead to confusion, loss of consciousness, convulsions.  
* **Heat stroke (exertional):** Triggered by intense physical activity in extreme heat; characterized by sweating, rhabdomyolysis, rapid heart rate, and core temperatures \>40°C.  
* **Rhabdomyolysis:** Mentioned as a feature of exertional heat stroke (definition not expanded on poster).  
* **SDOH:** Social determinants of health (housing, food security, financial support, social connectedness, healthcare access).  
* **Contact burns:** Burns from contact with extremely hot surfaces (e.g., pavement) after falls.  
* **Anticholinergics / SSRIs / TCAs:** Medication classes listed as potentially reducing heat tolerance (not defined on poster).  
* **NSAIDs:** Over-the-counter medications listed as impairing heat tolerance.

## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster argues that family context is a key leverage point for improving diet behaviors and cardiometabolic health among Hispanic/Latinx households. It summarizes three lines of work from the Community Nutrition and Cardiometabolic Health Laboratory: (1) determinants of diet quality, (2) the home food environment as a target for intervention, and (3) culturally relevant interventions. Findings highlighted include results from the Athletes for Life community/family trial (n=131 households) examining home food availability changes, and from a 10-week parenting intervention trial (n=235 dyads) plus observational associations between feeding practices and adolescent cardiometabolic risk factors (n=61 dyads). Overall, the poster concludes that focusing on family health and household context is a promising approach for promoting healthy eating among Hispanics.

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* Family matters because lifestyle behaviors are learned at home and shared; interventions can affect multiple family members; motivation includes being present for family; parents shape home food environment through roles and practices; family is central in Hispanic support structures.  
* Athletes for Life RCT (n=131 households) included home food environment content; bar charts show pre/post differences for intervention vs control in home availability of vegetables, fruit, prepared desserts, and sugar-sweetened beverages; “\*p\<0.05” is noted; and **no differences in change** for high-sugar breakfast cereals, candy, or sugar-free beverages.  
* Feeding practice associations (n=61 dyads): use of media during meals is associated with adolescent weight and BMI z-score; parental restrictive feeding style is associated with adolescent weight, BMI%, and DBP; parental energy index is associated with adolescent weight and BMI%.  
* Family meal frequency/fast food frequency/importance of family meals and inclusion of several foods/beverages during family meals were **not associated** with adolescent risk factors (as stated).  
* Conclusion: focusing on family health and broad household environmental context is a promising approach for promoting healthy eating among Hispanics.

## **P6. Discussion & implications**

* **Authors’ stated conclusions:** Focusing on family health and the broad household environmental context is a promising approach for promoting healthy eating among Hispanics.  
    
* **Implications (poster-grounded):**  
    
  * Interventions that modify the home food environment may influence availability of fruits/vegetables and sugar-containing foods in Hispanic households.  
  * Parenting practices and mealtime context (e.g., media use, restrictive feeding) may be meaningful targets for reducing adolescent cardiometabolic risk factors in Latinx families.  
  * Cultural sensitivity is emphasized: family plays a central role in support structures among Hispanic communities, suggesting family-based approaches are appropriate.


* **Mechanism/explanation (ONLY if stated):** Parents shape health behaviors by modeling behavior, providing resources, shaping the home food environment, setting rules/feeding practices, and influencing family meals and food availability.

## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster analyzes fecal shotgun metagenomic data from adults with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and typically developing (TD) controls collected before and after a microbiota transplant therapy (MTT) trial. Using a published list of globally prevalent codiversified (n=36) and non-codiversified (n=23) microbial species, the authors report that adults with ASD have a lower total relative abundance of codiversified microbes than TD controls. They further report that decreased ASD severity after MTT is associated with increased total relative abundance of codiversified microbes, while non-codiversified microbes do not predict ASD severity.

1. Use fecal shotgun metagenomic data collected **before and after** a double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase 2 MTT trial.  
2. Define two microbial sets from Suzuki et al. 2022: **36 codiversified** and **23 non-codiversified** globally prevalent microbial species.

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* Adults with ASD have a **lower total relative abundance of codiversified microbes** compared to TD controls (Wilcoxon rank sum test).  
* A decrease in ASD severity after MTT is associated with an **increase** in total relative abundance of codiversified microbes (Spearman rho; shown at Time point 1 vs Time point 2).  
* The total relative abundance of **non-codiversified** microbes does **not** predict ASD severity (Spearman rho; shown at Time point 1 and Time point 2).  
* Conclusion stated: Codiversifying microbes can serve as biomarkers of ASD status and severity.

## **P10. Glossary**

* **ASD:** Autism Spectrum Disorder.  
* **TD:** Typically developing.  
* **Gut–brain axis:** Bidirectional communication between the gut and the brain.  
* **Codiversification / co-diversification:** Phylogenetic congruence between humans and certain gut microbial species, with ancestry-specific strains maintained via transmission among genetically related individuals over long timescales.  
* **MTT:** Microbiota Transplant Therapy (Phase 2 trial referenced).  
* **Fecal shotgun metagenomics:** Sequencing-based method to profile microbial species in fecal samples (pipeline details not stated).  
* **CARS:** Childhood Autism Rating Scale (used here to measure ASD severity).  
* **Wilcoxon rank sum test:** Nonparametric test used for ASD vs TD comparison (exact p-value not readable).  
* **Spearman rho:** Rank correlation used to relate microbial abundance to ASD severity.

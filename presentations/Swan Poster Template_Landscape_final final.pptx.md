## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster proposes and tests the feasibility of a **12-week online mindful eating program** as a structured “off-ramp” for adults who discontinue GLP-1 receptor agonist therapy. The rationale is that GLP-1s reduce appetite and “food noise” during use, but discontinuation often leads to renewed food noise, dysregulated eating, and weight regain—creating “behavioral chaos” without a behavioral plan. The study is a randomized, controlled feasibility trial (intervention vs wait-list control with crossover), measuring feasibility (recruitment, retention, adherence, satisfaction) and preliminary efficacy outcomes including food noise (RAID-FN), eating-related distress, anxiety/depression symptoms, and quality of life, with follow-ups at 3 and 6 months.

## **P4. Methods (deep extraction)**

### **P4.1 Design & workflow**

1. Recruit nationally via social media/online platforms adults who discontinued GLP-1 receptor agonist therapy within 2–3 weeks and meet exclusion criteria (no diabetes; no active eating disorder).  
2. Randomize into a controlled feasibility trial with an immediate intervention group and a wait-list control group (usual care initially, then crossover).  
3. Deliver a 12-week online mindful eating program (12 weekly 90-minute group workshops) led by a trained facilitator, in a format similar to the Am I Hungry?® Mindful Eating Program.  
4. Collect assessments at baseline, post-intervention (12 weeks), 3 months, and 6 months.

## **P6. Discussion & implications**

* **Authors’ stated conclusions:** There are currently no evidence-based behavioral interventions designed as a clinically meaningful off-ramp following GLP-1 therapy; the presented mindful eating program is intended to provide structured support during discontinuation and inform future interventions reducing return to pre-medication behaviors.  
    
* **Implications (poster-grounded):**  
    
  * Clinicians may need a “transition plan” for patients discontinuing GLP-1s because discontinuation is common and often followed by return of food noise, dysregulated eating, and weight regain.  
  * A scalable, skills-based online group program could fill an identified clinical gap during GLP-1 discontinuation by targeting upstream cognitive/attentional mechanisms (food noise) linked to eating behavior.


* **Mechanism/explanation (ONLY if stated):** GLP-1s quiet physiology (reduce appetite signaling, dampen reward salience, reduce intrusive food thoughts), but do not build durable self-regulation skills; mindful eating strengthens top-down processes (attentional control, interoceptive awareness, flexible decision-making) needed after pharmacologic support is withdrawn.  
    
* **Connections to prior work (ONLY if referenced):** Poster includes eight references (KFF poll; JAMA Network Open discontinuation/reinitiation; STEP 1 extension; BMJ meta-analysis; Nutrients conceptual model; Cureus neurocircuit framing; Am I Hungry?® book; RAID-FN validation).

## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster presents a policy translation effort to expand breastfeeding and lactation support in Arizona by addressing gaps in Medicaid coverage for outpatient lactation services. Guided by a systems change framework, the team translated evidence on structural gaps into two bills: HB 2051 (Medicaid coverage of breastfeeding/lactation services across settings) and HB 2072 (state certification program for lactation care providers). The poster summarizes a policy gap review (Medicaid State Plan Amendments, 2012–2024) showing 14 states with explicit lactation coverage provisions and notes Arizona lacks formal Medicaid reimbursement mechanisms for outpatient lactation services, then tracks the legislative progress of both bills through early March 2026\.

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* A comprehensive SPA review (Jan 2012–Dec 2024\) identified **14 states** with explicit Medicaid provisions for lactation coverage and reimbursement.  
    
* The poster states **Arizona lacks formal Medicaid reimbursement mechanisms for outpatient lactation services**.  
    
* Two bills were developed to address gaps:  
    
  * **HB 2051:** Adds breastfeeding/lactation services across inpatient, outpatient, home-based, and group settings to AHCCCS medically necessary services contractors must cover.  
  * **HB 2072:** Establishes voluntary state certification for lactation care providers, with **two-year certifications** issued by AZDHS; defines scope of practice, competency standards, and certification requirements.


* Legislative progress (through early March 2026\) includes:  
    
  * HHS committee unanimous vote **12–0** on **Jan 22, 2026**.  
  * House floor steps: voice vote **Feb 5, 2026**; third read passage **Feb 17, 2026** with **HB 2051: 48–6** and **HB 2072: 48–9**.  
  * Senate first read completed; awaiting committee assignment; **HB 2051 assigned to Appropriations as of Mar 4, 2026**.


* Policy impact pathway (diagram): certification → provider recognition → Medicaid enrollment → reimbursement authority → expanded support → healthier families.

   * **A:** Establishes a voluntary state certification program for lactation care providers; AZDHS would issue two-year certifications and define scope, competency standards, and certification requirements.

8. **Q:** What is the intended “policy impact” pathway?  
     
   * **A:** Certification leads to provider recognition, enabling Medicaid enrollment and reimbursement authority, which expands lactation support and leads to healthier families.

## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster proposes an evolutionary framework to improve selectivity in integrin-targeted therapies by distinguishing **highly conserved RGD-binding cores** from **lineage-derived, paralog-specific regions** within the PS2 α-integrin group (αIIb, α5, αV, α8). Using orthologs from **27 vertebrate species** spanning about **450 million years**, the authors reconstruct a time-calibrated phylogeny, infer ancestral sequences, and map conservation/divergence signals onto structural/functional domains (including the αIIb–β3 ligand-binding pocket). They conclude that many adverse effects (e.g., bleeding) may arise from drugs binding conserved interfaces shared across paralogs, and that targeting αIIb-specific derived regions could improve selectivity while preserving necessary shared functions.

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* PS2 integrins originated before vertebrate evolution; four paralogs diverged through stepwise duplication during early vertebrate evolution.  
* The MRCA of PS2 integrins likely functioned as a broadly expressed **RGD-binding ECM receptor**; conserved regions may explain compensatory signaling among paralogs.  
* The platelet-restricted fibrinogen-binding function of **αIIb** is a derived innovation linked to vertebrate hemostasis.  
* Excessive bleeding and off-target effects in integrin-directed therapies likely arise from non-selective binding to conserved RGD-binding interfaces shared across paralogs.  
* Targeting **αIIb-specific, lineage-derived regions** may improve selectivity and reduce bleeding-related adverse effects.  
* Poster figures and table support these claims by combining: a time-calibrated phylogeny (Figure 1), a functional presence/absence table with ancestral node inference (Table 1), and structural/sequence mapping of binding-region substitutions (Figure 2).

## **P6. Discussion & implications**

* **Authors’ stated conclusions:** Integrin-directed therapy failures (bleeding, limited selectivity, compensatory signaling, context-dependent effects) are likely linked to targeting conserved RGD-binding interfaces shared across PS2 paralogs; precision targeting should instead prioritize αIIb-specific lineage-derived regions to reduce bleeding risk while improving selectivity.  
    
* **Implications (poster-grounded):**  
    
  * Evolutionary constraint metrics may help prioritize safer, more selective drug targets within integrin families.  
  * Structural mapping of lineage-specific substitutions can identify candidate regions for subtype-selective targeting strategies.


* **Mechanism/explanation (ONLY if stated):** Compensatory signaling among paralogs likely arises from conserved regions inherited from an ancestral broadly expressed RGD-binding receptor; off-target effects arise from non-selective binding to conserved interfaces.  
    
* **Connections to prior work (ONLY if referenced):** References include Campbell et al. 2011; Slack et al. 2020; Hughes 2011; Johnson et al. 2009 (PMIDs provided).

## **P10. Glossary**

* **Integrins:** Cell-adhesion transmembrane receptors mediating cell–cell and cell–ECM interactions.  
* **ECM:** Extracellular matrix.  
* **PS2 α-integrin group:** Set of α integrin paralogs highlighted here: αIIb, α5, αV, α8, sharing conserved RGD-binding capability but diverging in ligand preference and tissue distribution.  
* **RGD:** Arg–Gly–Asp motif (ligand-binding capability referenced as conserved across PS2 group).  
* **αIIb:** Platelet-restricted integrin; mediates fibrinogen-mediated clot formation.  
* **α5:** Primary fibronectin receptor involved in matrix assembly.  
* **αV:** Broadly expressed integrin central to angiogenesis and tumor biology.  
* **α8:** Tissue-specific integrin important in development/organ-specific adhesion.  
* **Paralogs:** Genes related by duplication events within a genome (here, the four PS2 α-integrins).  
* **MRCA:** Most recent common ancestor.  
* **Mya:** Million years ago (time scale used in phylogeny).  
* **Ancestral sequence reconstruction:** Inference of ancestral protein sequences at internal nodes to identify conserved vs lineage-specific substitutions.  
* **Maximum parsimony (trait inference):** Method used to reconstruct ancestral functional annotations in Table 1\.

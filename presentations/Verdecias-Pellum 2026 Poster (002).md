## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster presents a qualitative multiple case study evaluating whether it is feasible to integrate health-related social needs (HRSN) screening and referral pathways into diabetes self-management education (DSME) delivered in community-based settings. Semi-structured interviews with DSME facilitators and organizational leaders across three urban and rural community-based organizations highlight challenges in current DSME structures—especially limited flexibility to address real-time social needs, absence of systematic screening/referral, and resource strain. The poster also identifies opportunities: DSME intake/enrollment as a practical moment for brief screening and referral planning, and community partnerships that support sustainability through shared responsibilities and trust. The conclusion is that integrating HRSN screening into DSME workflows is feasible and valuable, but requires navigator/facilitator training, cross-sector partnerships, and reimbursement mechanisms.

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* DSME programs improve health behaviors, but HRSN (e.g., food insecurity, housing instability, transportation) can hinder DSME engagement and outcomes; many DSME programs do not systematically assess HRSN.  
    
* **Challenges in current DSME structure:**  
    
  * Rigid DSME structure limits flexibility to address real-time HRSN.  
  * Absent screening and referral processes leave facilitators unsure how to respond to clients’ needs.  
  * Added responsibilities without resources strain capacity.  
  * Unmet HRSN drive attrition and may exclude more vulnerable clients.


* **Opportunities for HRSN screening:**  
    
  * DSME intake and enrollment are practical moments for brief HRSN screening and referral planning.  
  * Community partnerships enable shared responsibilities and strengthen sustainability.  
  * Building on community trust can improve engagement and retention.


* A facilitator quote emphasizes that participants may stop attending due to unmet life needs (“…it’s just…interest…survival.”).  
    
* **Conclusion:** Integrating HRSN screening into DSME workflows is feasible and valuable while maintaining program fidelity; training navigators/DSME facilitators supports early identification and referrals; cross-sector partnerships and reimbursement mechanisms are key to sustain integration; CBOs are an ideal setting to address HRSN while providing DSME support.

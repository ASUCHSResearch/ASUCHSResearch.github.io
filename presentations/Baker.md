## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster describes SSNE-STaR, an AHRQ/PCORI-funded Learning Health System Center designed to build embedded CER/PCOR capacity within safety net organizations serving medically underserved populations. The program uses three cores (Administration, Research Education, Research Data Analysis) plus structured mentorship (ASU research mentor \+ clinical mentor) and “Rapid Implementers” to help scholars disseminate findings into practice. Early results report 18 enrolled scholars (16 active) embedded across Arizona safety net systems—exceeding the original 12-scholar target—suggesting strong demand and growing cross-institutional collaboration.

## **P4. Methods (deep extraction)**

### **P4.1 Design & workflow**

1. SSNE-STaR is organized into three interconnected cores to support scholars: Administration Core, Research Education Core (REC), and Research Data Analysis Core (RDAC).  
2. Each scholar is paired with two mentors: an ASU Research Mentor (CER/PCOR methods) and a Clinical Mentor (real-world application in healthcare settings); mentors meet monthly with scholars.  
3. Each scholar develops and implements a CER/PCOR project of their choosing; in Year 2, “Rapid Implementers” collaborate to disseminate findings into practice aligned with underserved population needs.

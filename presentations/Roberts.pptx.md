## **P2. 30-second summary**

This mouse study tests whether an **aged gut microbiota**, independent of the mouse’s chronological age, worsens recovery after femoral fracture. Mice underwent **microbiome depletion with an antibiotic cocktail** followed by **fecal microbiota transplant (FMT)** from either **young (12-week)** or **aged (18-month)** donors, then received a **left femoral fracture** with outcomes assessed **21 days post-fracture**. The key findings are sex-specific: in **female mice**, aged-FMT is associated with **slower recovery of pain-related behaviors**, **reduced callus bone metrics** (BV, BV/TV, BMD), **higher inflammatory/pain-related gene expression in DRG**, and **increased CD3⁺ T-cell infiltration in DRG**; males show minimal or no differences across most outcomes.

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* Fracture is associated with dramatic alterations in gut microbiota composition; stacked-bar plots show top-10 genera changes from **Day 0** to **Days 3, 10, 14, 18** in young and aged mice.  
* Young vs aged microbiota **does not significantly affect body weight recovery** following fracture; both sexes show an initial weight drop after fracture and recovery to baseline by **day 21**; “no significant main effects of microbiome age.”  
* **Pain behaviors:** In females, young microbiome pain/nociception measures return to pre-fracture levels faster than aged microbiota; young-FMT accelerates recovery vs aged-FMT. Males show minimal differences.  
* **Healing:** In females, aged-FMT delays fracture healing and decreases callus bone content; aged-FMT females have significantly lower BV, BV/TV, and BMD vs young-FMT (\*P\<0.05). Male outcomes not shown; described as not significant.  
* **DRG gene expression:** Female aged-FMT shows higher expression of inflammation-related genes **Il10, Mcp1, Csf1r, Ccl20** and altered regulation of pain-related genes **Ngf1 and Npy** vs young-FMT; in males, only **Ccl20** differed (\*P\<0.05).  
* **Serum cytokines:** Sex-specific cytokine/chemokine responses following fracture; females show higher TNFα at day 7 vs day 21; males show greater TNFα at day 7 and significantly increased SDF1 at day 21\.  
* **Immune infiltration:** Aged microbiome increases DRG **CD3⁺ T cells** at **day 7** post-fracture; no significant difference in **CD11b⁺** monocyte/macrophage populations.

## **P2. 30-second summary**

This cross-sectional study used an anonymous online survey of adults with self-reported clinician-diagnosed celiac disease (n=327) to examine whether food skills and psychological resilience are associated with gluten-free diet (GFD) adherence. Correlational screening identified food skills (meal planning, food preparation, food safety), resilience, and age as variables linked to adherence. In multiple linear regression, meal planning and resilience remained significant explanatory variables, jointly explaining 12.3% of variance in GFD adherence scores, suggesting interventions focused on meal-planning skills and resilience-building may improve adherence.

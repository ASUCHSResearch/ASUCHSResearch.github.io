## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster is a high-level overview of the Sweazea lab’s research themes under “Nutritional and Metabolic Resilience,” spanning comparative physiology, One Health food environments, and dietary interventions for metabolic disease. It highlights birds as long-lived vertebrates with unusually high blood sugar and lipids yet low diabetes/cancer, motivating research into protective mechanisms and natural insulin resistance. It also summarizes analyses of \~2,000 food items showing glycemic index/load do not differ across NOVA processing categories, while minimally processed foods show higher total antioxidant content than processed/ultra-processed foods. Finally, it notes lab work on Western diet contributions to metabolic syndrome/diabetes and exploration of a novel fulvic acid supplement as an intervention.

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* Birds live longer and healthier lives than mammals of similar body mass despite having the highest blood sugar and lipid concentrations among vertebrates, motivating research into how they avoid diabetes and cancer.  
* Analysis of **1,995** common food items found glycemic index and glycemic load do **not differ** across NOVA food processing levels.  
* Analysis of **1,946** food items supports that minimally processed foods have **higher total antioxidant content** than processed or ultra-processed foods.  
* Ongoing work examines wild bird food choice across an urban–rural gradient and potential impacts of poor nutrition in the food chain (IACUC protocol provided).  
* Western diets high in saturated fats, simple carbohydrates, and salt are described as major contributors to metabolic syndrome and diabetes; the lab is exploring a novel fulvic acid supplement to mitigate symptoms.

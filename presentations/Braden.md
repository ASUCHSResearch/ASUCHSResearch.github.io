## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster investigates biological and behavioral signs of increased neurodegenerative vulnerability in aging autistic adults using multimodal evidence: MRI cortical morphometry linked to executive/motor/language performance, Parkinson’s disease (PD) polygenic risk and clinician-rated parkinsonism, and Alzheimer’s disease (AD)-related blood biomarkers plus longitudinal verbal memory. The authors report greater age-related frontal–motor cortical surface area loss in autistic adults and that smaller orbitofrontal surface area relates to worse executive performance, slower finger tapping, and lower semantic fluency. Autistic adults show higher PD polygenic risk and higher clinician-rated parkinsonism severity, and they show steeper longitudinal decline in long-term verbal memory with specific proteomic differences (CRP up; NRGN and RUVBL2 down).

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* Background claim: Middle-aged and older autistic adults are **2.6×** more likely to be diagnosed with early-onset Alzheimer’s disease and **6×** more likely to be diagnosed with Parkinson’s disease than non-autistic adults.  
    
* **Brain structure \+ behavior (Aim 1):**  
    
  * Autistic adults show greater age-related surface area loss in left frontal (orbitofrontal/inferior frontal) and motor (precentral) regions.  
  * In autistic adults, smaller orbitofrontal surface area correlates with worse executive performance and lower fluency / slower motor speed (correlation coefficients and p-values shown).


* **Parkinsonism (Aim 2):**  
    
  * Autistic adults show significantly higher PD polygenic risk (PD-PGS) than non-autistic adults (t(134)=2.98, p=0.003).  
  * Autistic adults show significantly higher clinician-rated parkinsonism severity (MDS-UPDRS Part III) than non-autistic adults (t(25)=-2.46, p=0.021), with large effect size (Cohen’s d=0.98).


* **Memory \+ blood biomarkers (Aim 3):**  
    
  * Autistic adults show steeper longitudinal decline in long-term verbal memory; multilevel model shows group-by-time interaction p=0.034.  
  * Proteomics: CRP upregulated (log2 FC=0.133, FDR p=0.013); NRGN downregulated (log2 FC=-0.144, FDR p=0.011); RUVBL2 downregulated (log2 FC=-0.073, FDR p=0.011).  
  * Poster also states elevated Aβ42 with modest individual discrimination (no numeric metrics provided).


* **Conclusion:** Across MRI, genomic, and proteomic modalities, findings point to frontal–motor system vulnerability and elevated neurodegenerative risk in aging autism.

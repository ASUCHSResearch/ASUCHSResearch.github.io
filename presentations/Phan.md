## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster describes three applications of **unbiased metagenomic sequencing** and bioinformatics to detect viruses and infer **evolutionary history and transmission routes** across environmental matrices, including **mosquito pools**, **dust/air samples**, and **dust accumulation on electronic devices**. The mosquito surveillance work (in collaboration with Maricopa County Vector Control and ASU collaborators) uses pooled mosquitoes (10–50 per pool) and visualizes viral families detected as sequence-read heatmaps, while dust studies include clinic waiting-area air sampling and comparative dust sources (Phoenix garden dust, indoor robotic vacuum dust, passive air sampler dust) visualized in a treemap. A third line of work examines how environmental factors influence dust—and potentially virus—accumulation on phones/tablets, with ongoing metagenomic sequencing experiments under controlled conditions.

## **P10. Glossary**

* **Metagenomics (unbiased):** Sequencing-based approach to detect genetic material from all organisms in a sample without targeting a specific pathogen (as framed in poster).  
* **Bioinformatic analyses:** Computational methods used to classify sequences and infer viral families/genomes.  
* **Vector:** Organism that transmits pathogens (mosquitoes described as vectors for West Nile virus and Zika virus).  
* **Virus family:** Taxonomic grouping used for classification (columns in Fig 1).  
* **Treemap:** Visualization showing proportional composition of categories (kingdom-level categories in Fig 3).  
* **Pool sample:** Combined sample of multiple organisms (10–50 mosquitoes per pool).  
* **AC/DC field:** Electrical field conditions mentioned as influencing dust accumulation on devices.  
* **Control pathogen:** Pathogen added in the device dust experiment design (identity not stated).

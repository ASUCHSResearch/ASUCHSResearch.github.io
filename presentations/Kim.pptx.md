## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster tests whether **intermittent fasting (IF)** can restore **leptin sensitivity** in diet-induced obese mice by increasing hypothalamic leptin receptor (**LepRb**) expression through **epigenetic regulation**. Male C57BL/6J mice were made obese on a high-fat diet, then assigned to **ad libitum** feeding or **alternate-day fasting** for 10–20 days depending on experiment, and leptin responsiveness was assessed via leptin injections, food intake/body weight, and hypothalamic signaling (pSTAT3). The authors report that IF improves leptin sensitivity without changing overall body weight or fat mass, increases hypothalamic LepRb and leptin signaling, and is associated with reduced repressive histone methylation (H3K27 methylation) at the LepRb promoter, mediated by JMJD3.

## **P4. Methods (deep extraction)**

### **P4.1 Design & workflow**

1. Induce diet-induced obesity: 7-week-old male C57BL/6J mice fed HFD (60% kcal from fat) for 10 weeks.  
     
2. Randomize mice to AL vs IF:  
     
   * IF \= alternate-day fasting (24h fast, 24h feed) for 10 days (5 cycles) or 20 days (10 cycles).

   

3. Assess leptin responsiveness:  
     
   * Leptin i.p. injection (0.25 mg/kg) at 6:00 PM and 12:00 AM daily for 3 days; record body weight and food intake.

   

4. Measure hypothalamic signaling and gene regulation:  
     
   * Immunostaining for pSTAT3 (Bregma \-1.46 mm) and JMJD3 (Bregma \-1.5 mm); quantify signal in ARC and VMH.  
   * qPCR for LepRb and histone demethylases; ChIP-PCR/qPCR for H3K27 methylation at LepRb promoter.

   

5. Test mediation by JMJD3:  
     
   * Bilateral stereotaxic microinjection into mediobasal hypothalamus of AAV expressing JMJD3 shRNA vs control shRNA (GFP-U6 constructs).  
   * After recovery, repeat HFD and AL/IF cycle experiment; assess LepRb expression and H3K27me2/3 at LepRb promoter.

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* IF improves leptin sensitivity in obese mice **without altering overall body weight or fat mass**.  
* IF enhances hypothalamic LepRb expression and leptin signaling (increased STAT3 phosphorylation and target gene activation).  
* Epigenetic analysis shows reduced H3K27 methylation at the LepRb promoter, suggesting transcriptional activation under IF.  
* IF modulates JMJD3 expression and hypothalamic H3K27m3 modification; JMJD3 expression changes across fed vs 24h fasted vs 6h re-fed states.  
* JMJD3 knockdown blunts IF-induced LepRb upregulation and histone demethylation at the LepRb promoter, supporting a mediating role of JMJD3.

## **P6. Discussion & implications**

* **Authors’ stated conclusions:** (1) IF improves leptin sensitivity in obese mice without altering overall body weight or fat mass. (2) IF enhances hypothalamic LepRb expression and leptin signaling (increased STAT3 phosphorylation and target gene activation). (3) IF reduces H3K27 methylation at LepRb promoter, consistent with transcriptional activation. (4) IF is supported as a dietary approach to reverse leptin resistance and restore metabolic balance in obesity.  
    
* **Implications (poster-grounded):**  
    
  * IF may counter leptin resistance by changing hypothalamic gene regulation rather than by reducing body weight alone.  
  * JMJD3-mediated epigenetic remodeling is presented as a potentially targetable mechanism for nutrition/pharmacology strategies aimed at obesity/metabolic dysfunction.


* **Mechanism/explanation (ONLY if stated):** IF reduces repressive H3K27me3 at the LepRb promoter (via JMJD3 demethylase), increasing LepRb expression and leptin signaling responsiveness.

## **P10. Glossary**

* **Leptin:** Hormone produced by adipose tissue that suppresses appetite and increases energy expenditure; leptin resistance occurs in obesity.  
* **Leptin resistance:** Hypothalamic unresponsiveness to leptin signaling contributing to weight gain/metabolic dysfunction.  
* **IF:** Intermittent fasting; here, alternate-day fasting (24h fast/24h feed).  
* **AL:** Ad libitum feeding (free access to food).  
* **HFD:** High-fat diet (60% kcal from fat).  
* **LepRb:** Long form leptin receptor in hypothalamus; target for transcriptional regulation in this study.  
* **STAT3 / pSTAT3:** Signaling protein phosphorylated downstream of LepRb activation; used as a leptin signaling readout.  
* **H3K27me3 / H3K27 methylation:** Repressive histone methylation mark associated with gene silencing; measured at LepRb promoter.  
* **JMJD3:** Histone demethylase implicated in removing H3K27 methylation; proposed mediator of IF effects.  
* **ChIP-PCR / ChIP-qPCR:** Chromatin immunoprecipitation with PCR quantification to measure histone marks at specific genomic regions.  
* **ARC:** Arcuate nucleus (hypothalamic region).  
* **VMH:** Ventromedial hypothalamus (hypothalamic region).  
* **AAV:** Adeno-associated virus vector used to deliver shRNA constructs.  
* **shRNA:** Short hairpin RNA used for gene knockdown (JMJD3 shRNA vs scramble control).

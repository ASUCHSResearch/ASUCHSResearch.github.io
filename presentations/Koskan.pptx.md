## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster describes a two-phase project using **digital storytelling (DST)** to address **COVID-19 vaccine hesitancy among Hispanic parents**, guided by the **Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB)**. In Phase I, **10 formerly hesitant** parents created digital stories and a community advisory board selected the top **4** for use. In Phase II, **80** Hispanic parents were randomized to DST vs control videos, with surveys at baseline, immediate post-test, and **2-month** follow-up; results suggest DST increased vaccine intention more than control (Δ **\+1.2 vs \+0.6**) and parents who viewed DSTs were **more likely** to vaccinate their children (reported **OR=5.20**, **RR=3.10**) and at follow-up **50% vs 16%** vaccinated (DST vs control).

## **P10. Glossary**

* **DST:** Digital storytelling; narrative videos created by parents to share experiences and influence beliefs/behavior.  
* **TPB:** Theory of Planned Behavior; theoretical framework guiding the study; poster notes TPB constructs assessed.  
* **Vaccine hesitancy:** Reluctance or delay in vaccination; cited as key contributor to lower COVID-19 vaccine uptake among Hispanic children.  
* **Community health workers:** Community partners involved throughout recruitment and retention.  
* **Community Advisory Board (CAB):** Group that selected the top 4 digital stories for intervention.  
* **OR:** Odds ratio; reported as 5.20 for likelihood of vaccinating children.  
* **RR:** Relative risk; reported as 3.10 for likelihood of vaccinating children.

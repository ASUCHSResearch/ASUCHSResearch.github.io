## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster is a **lab portfolio overview** summarizing four ongoing/related research areas in the Racette Lab focused on **cardiometabolic health, Alzheimer’s disease-related interventions, Native American cardiovascular health, and healthspan-extension approaches**. It highlights (1) a time-restricted eating feasibility trial in mild cognitive impairment (MCI) with a ClinicalTrials.gov identifier, (2) a study of acute aerobic exercise effects with/without anti-amyloid therapy in MCI due to Alzheimer’s disease, (3) cardiovascular health measurement work in Native American youth/adults using CIMT and DXA, and (4) an “XPRIZE Healthspan” effort including multi-baric therapy and AI-driven exercise.

## **P10. Glossary**

* **TRE:** Time-restricted eating.  
* **MCI:** Mild cognitive impairment.  
* **BNI:** Barrow Neurological Institute.  
* **Anti-amyloid therapy:** Therapy targeting amyloid beta (mentioned as combined with aerobic exercise in Study \#2).  
* **CIMT:** Carotid intima media thickness (assessed by carotid ultrasound to assess atherosclerosis risk).  
* **DXA:** Dual-energy x-ray absorptiometry (used for body composition).  
* **Healthspan:** Period of life spent in good health; used in poster framing (Study \#4).  
* **XPRIZE Healthspan:** A 7-year, $101 million global competition focused on approaches to reduce biological age and increase healthspan.  
* **Hypobaric/Hyperbaric therapy:** Pressure-based therapies listed as methods in Study \#4.  
* **SmartFit Method:** Personalized AI-driven exercise approach listed in Study \#4 (details not stated on poster).

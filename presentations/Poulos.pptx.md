## **P2. 30-second summary**

This concurrent mixed-methods study in **5 Phoenix Metro (AZ) elementary schools** examined recess during **extreme heat**, focusing on (1) practices/costs of offering recess, and children’s (2) readiness to learn and (3) movement by heat exposure and recess setting. Data included **15 interviews**, **981 questionnaires over 6 weeks**, and **91 recess observations plus 5 thermal assessments over 5 weeks**, among **5th graders (age 10–11; n=218)** and staff (**n=15**). Results show air temperatures **91–109°F (33–43°C)** and mean radiant temperatures **103°F (39°C) in tree shade to 163°F (73°C) on courts**; readiness to learn was higher when recess was **outside or in gyms vs classrooms (p\<.05)**, and MVPA was **17% higher outdoors** and **40% higher in gyms** compared to classroom recess (**p\<.001**), where children were mainly sedentary (**71%**).

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* Air temperatures ranged from **91–109°F (33–43°C)**.  
    
* Heat exposure (mean radiant temperature) ranged from **103°F (39°C)** under tree shade to **163°F (73°C)** on courts.  
    
* **Aim 1:** Essential resources to maintain safe outdoor recess (e.g., shade structures, water bottle filling stations) required years of fundraising.  
    
* **Aim 2:** Readiness to learn was higher when recess was **outside or in gyms** compared to **classrooms** (**p\<.05**).  
    
* **Aim 3:** Recess setting distribution: **60% outside**, **20% classrooms**, **20% gyms**.  
    
* **Aim 3:** Students were moderate-to-vigorously active **17% more outdoors** and **40% more in gyms** compared to classroom recess (**p\<.001**), where children were mainly sedentary (**71%**).  
    
* Implication themes:  
    
  * Schools want more shade and dedicated indoor movement spaces; infrastructure is expensive.  
  * Kids are least ready and mostly sitting when recess is inside classrooms; gyms could yield \~5× more MVPA than classrooms and 2× more than outside (stated as a scenario).  
  * Most active kids remain vigorous outside despite heat.  
  * Outdoor spaces where kids are most active are generally least shaded.

## **P6. Discussion & implications**

* **Authors’ stated conclusions:** Schools face expensive infrastructure needs to support safe recess during extreme heat; keeping recess inside classrooms is associated with lower readiness to learn and high sedentary behavior; gyms and outdoor recess are associated with higher readiness and greater MVPA, and shade placement may not align with the most active outdoor spaces.  
    
* **Implications (poster-grounded):**  
    
  * Investment priorities: schoolyard shade and dedicated indoor movement spaces (e.g., gyms) may support both learning readiness and physical activity during heat.  
  * Practice guidance need: lack of uniform extreme-heat recess guidance leads to variable practices; evidence supports reconsidering “classroom recess” defaults.

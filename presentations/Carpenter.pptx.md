## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster describes a micro-randomized trial (MRT) feasibility study aimed at optimizing a just-in-time adaptive intervention (JITAI) to increase wear time of a wrist-worn alcohol biosensor (Skyn) among young adults with HIV. The team tested engagement strategies at onboarding (standard vs engagement), using reciprocity rewards and personalized feedback delivered through a gamified smartphone app plus message strategy, while collecting daily ecological momentary assessments (EMAs). Preliminary results show 20% overall attrition after baseline and substantially higher attrition in the standard condition (37%) than in the engagement condition (17%); retention was associated with several person-level factors including income, sensation-seeking, mental health symptom severity, and extraversion.

## **P4. Methods (deep extraction)**

### **P4.1 Design & workflow**

1. Baseline/onboarding: 49 young adults (18–29) with HIV completed baseline session and were onboarded into either a Standard (69%) or Engagement (31%) condition.

   * Participants wore the Skyn wrist biosensor measuring TAC.

## **P5. Results (exhaustive)**

### **P5.1 Key findings (bullet list)**

* Baseline sample: **n=49** young adults (18–29) with HIV completed baseline session.  
    
* Onboarding condition distribution: **Standard 69%** vs **Engagement 31%**.  
    
* Attrition: **20% attrition following baseline** (overall).  
    
* Attrition differs by condition: **Standard 37%** vs **Engagement 17%**.  
    
* Reasons for attrition:  
    
  * **44%** stopped responding  
  * **12%** unstable housing leading to lost/stolen equipment  
  * **16%** difficult financial situation / concern about compensation schedule  
  * **28%** difficulty with sensor and/or smartphone, or no reason given


* Person-level factors associated with retention (Fisher’s exact test p-values):  
    
  * Annual household income distribution differs by retention status (p=0.035)  
  * Sensation seeking higher in non-retained (4.2 ± 0.7) vs retained (3.1 ± 1.0), p=0.024  
  * DSM-5 mental health disorder symptoms higher in non-retained (27.9 ± 14.1) vs retained (15.9 ± 13.2), p=0.016  
  * Big-5 Extraversion higher in non-retained (12.2 ± 1.9) vs retained (9.2 ± 2.4), p\<0.001  
  * Marginal findings (†): sexual sensation seeking p=0.063; cocaine use p=0.063; PSQI p=0.086


* Conclusions: Retention was lower without engagement strategies; person-level factors associated with retention; ongoing MRT and usability analyses underway.

## **P10. Glossary**

* **Engagement:** Investment of energies—affective (moods/emotions/well-being), cognitive (attention/information processing), physical (performing a task).  
* **JITAI:** Just-in-time adaptive intervention; adapts to rapidly changing events and provides support at the right time “in the wild.”  
* **MRT:** Micro-randomized trial; repeatedly randomizes intervention options at multiple time points to learn what works when/for whom.  
* **EMA:** Ecological momentary assessment; repeated real-time assessments of internal/external states.  
* **Skyn biosensor:** Wrist-worn alcohol use biosensor measuring transdermal alcohol concentration (TAC).  
* **TAC:** Transdermal alcohol concentration (alcohol from skin).  
* **Reciprocity:** Small no-strings-attached reward intended to activate a tendency to return favors.  
* **PSQI:** Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index.  
* **NCT05431855:** ClinicalTrials.gov identifier listed on the poster.

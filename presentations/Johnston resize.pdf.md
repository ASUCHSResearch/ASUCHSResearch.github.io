## **P2. 30-second summary**

This randomized controlled trial tested whether **spirulina supplementation (3 g/day for 8 weeks)** improves mood and cognitive performance in healthy adults compared with placebo. Mood was assessed using the **POMS questionnaire** (six subscales), and cognition using the **Trail Making Test (TMT-A, TMT-B, and TMT B–A)**. Results suggest **modest improvements**: some mood subscales show significant pre/post time effects, **Total POMS** shows a trend in the spirulina group (**paired t-test p=0.074**), and **cognitive flexibility (TMT B–A) improved** with a significant 8-week change difference (**p=0.025**), though **between-group interactions were not significant** by repeated-measures ANOVA (**p\>0.05**).

2. Randomize to **spirulina 3 g/day** or placebo, taken daily for **8 weeks**.

### **P4.2 Measures & instruments**

  * **What it measures:** Mood over the past week (including current day) across six categories.  
  * **Scale/units:** 65 brief phrases; 5-point Likert (“not at all” to “extremely”).  
  * **Thresholds/cutoffs:** Not stated on poster.

* **Measure:** Trail Making Test (TMT)  
    
  * **What it measures:** Cognitive flexibility (shifting), visual attention, and task switching.  
      
  * **Scale/units:** Time in **seconds** (timed test).

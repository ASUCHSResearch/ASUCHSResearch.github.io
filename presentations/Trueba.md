## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster evaluates a Parkinson’s disease (PD) community education “open house” delivered in a hybrid format (in-person at ASU Tempe campus clinic and live via Zoom). The project aimed to describe how people with PD, care partners, and professionals perceived the event and to identify benefits, unmet needs, and priorities for future education initiatives. Survey responses before and after the event suggest increased familiarity with therapy options and where to find help—especially among people with PD and care partners—while professionals’ ratings remained stable. Qualitative comments emphasized appreciation for concrete information, social connection, and exposure to specific programs such as SPEAK OUT\! and group offerings.

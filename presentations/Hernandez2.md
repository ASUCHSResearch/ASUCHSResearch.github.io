## **P2. 30-second summary**

This poster reports preliminary findings from structured qualitative interviews with trainees in ASU’s Community Health Worker (CHW) Training Program who previously worked as Behavioral Health Technicians (BHTs), peer recovery support specialists (PRSS), or other behavioral health paraprofessionals. The study examines how these trainees perceive CHW roles relative to their prior roles, including overlap, boundaries, training requirements, and workforce experiences when cross-trained. With 9 participants interviewed so far, respondents describe major variability in how CHWs are positioned within healthcare organizations, frequent role ambiguity and limited recognition, and workforce sustainability concerns (grant-funded instability, inadequate pay), suggesting a need for clearer role delineation, standardized training pathways, and expanded reimbursement mechanisms.

## **P6. Discussion & implications**

* **Authors’ stated conclusions:** Participants described substantial variability in how CHWs are positioned within healthcare organizations, including differences in authority, recognition, and collaboration with behavioral health and peer support professionals. Role ambiguity and unclear role definitions may limit effective integration into multidisciplinary teams. Findings suggest the need for clearer role delineation, standardized training pathways, and stronger institutional recognition, with workforce sustainability supported by expanded reimbursement mechanisms, stronger career advancement pathways, and fair/consistent compensation.  
    
* **Implications (poster-grounded):**  
    
  * Clarifying boundaries between CHWs, BHTs, and PRSS could reduce role confusion and improve team integration.  
  * Sustainable funding/reimbursement and standardized training may stabilize the CHW workforce and reduce burnout.  
  * Improving CHW workforce stability may enhance integration into healthcare/public health systems and improve community health outcomes.


* **Mechanism/explanation (ONLY if stated):** Organizational barriers and unclear role definitions limit integration; improved reimbursement and recognition support workforce stability and integration.  
    
* **Connections to prior work (ONLY if referenced):** References include national CHW role/training profile study (Ingram et al., 2012), CHW sustainability laws and reimbursement (Schmit et al., 2021), and systematic review of community-based health worker programs (Scott et al., 2018).
